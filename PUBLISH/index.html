That's okay. Most people won't tell you that "I don't know" is the most honest answer a lot of 18-year-olds have — and it doesn't mean you're behind. This section isn't here to push you anywhere. It's here to make your options visible.

Three questions to help you decide

Do you need to earn money soon, or do you have some time?

Do you have your matric certificate?

Do you prefer working with people, things, or ideas?

Understanding your options

Studying

3–4+ years committed to a qualification. Opens certain doors but costs time and money. University is not the only path to a good career.

Learnerships

Earn a stipend while learning on the job and come out with a qualification. Underused and underrated in South Africa.

Short courses

A few months to learn a skill — coding, design, bookkeeping — and start earning without committing to a 4-year degree.

Intentional gap time

Volunteering, building something, or getting work experience can be more valuable than rushing into a decision you're not ready for.

Know something that should be here?
